Institutional IT

EMIS — IOE Purwanchal Campus

As IT Officer at IOE Purwanchal Campus (Tribhuvan University), I have designed and developed a comprehensive Education Management Information System to digitise every major administrative and academic workflow. Below are the 15 systems built and deployed in-house.

Student Services
07
Online Admission Portal
Prospective students fill the admission form, upload documents, and pay the application fee entirely online. Verified applications feed directly into the admissions queue — no redundant data entry, no campus visit required for submission.
05
Voucher / Token Submission & Auto-Validation
Students upload their bank voucher or token number through their account. The backend validates the payment against bank records automatically and posts the confirmed token to the finance system — eliminating fee-submission queues and providing a clean audit trail.
04
Hostel Allocation System (Dynamic Rank-Based)
Students fill a single application form and the system ranks each applicant dynamically using the campus criteria — academic merit, distance from home, gender quota, and special categories. Seats are allocated from the ranked list transparently, removing manual preparation and disputes.
03
Automatic Clearance Form Generation
On a clearance request, the system queries every relevant section — library, hostel, store, finance, and departments — and assembles the form with each section's live status. Pending dues are flagged; signing officers only verify and approve.
02
Certificate Generation & Graduate Tracking
On-request generation of campus certificates (character, transcript, provisional, migration) directly from the student records. Graduated students are tracked longitudinally — employment, further studies, contact — building an alumni database for outcome reporting and accreditation.
Academic Management
11
Exam Management with Auto Seat-Planning
Takes subject / room / candidate list as input and automatically generates the full seat plan and invigilator assignments, factoring in room capacity, attendance, and faculty availability. Produces all official letters — invigilation duties, room allotments, candidate lists, seating charts — ready for signature. Reduced exam planning from days to minutes.
14
Routine Management & Teaching-Tracking Portal
Campus Chief, Vice Chief, and section heads log in and flag each scheduled class as Taught or Not Taught. Teachers record attendance on the same day — no back-dated entries permitted. Supports one-click download of master, department-wise, individual teacher, and personal routines.
17
Marksheet Aggregation & Performance Audit System
Ingests all student marksheets and segregates them by batch, faculty, programme, semester, and subject. Generates audit reports — subject-wise pass/fail rates, mark distributions, top and bottom performers, and cross-batch trend lines — used for academic review and accreditation evidence.
13
Canva for Educator — Free PRO Access
Provisioned Canva for Education (Educator) access campus-wide, enabling all students and staff to use Canva PRO features — design tools for coursework, presentations, reports, and departmental material — at no individual cost.
IT Infrastructure
06
Centralised Campus Networking
Campus-wide network placing every academic block, lab, and administrative section on a unified managed network with consistent IP planning, VLAN segmentation, and central monitoring. Forms the backbone for all other EMIS systems.
10
Centralised Server Management
A unified administration layer for all campus servers — virtualisation hosts, application servers, database servers, storage and backup nodes — with a single pane for monitoring, patching, backup verification, and access control. The EMIS Cell registers domains, provisions virtual servers, deploys web applications, and hosts student and departmental sites.
18
GPU Resource Allocation System (Time-Shared)
Scheduling and remote-access platform letting students and researchers reserve GPU time on campus compute servers on a time-sharing basis. Includes queuing, usage logs, and quota controls — supporting AI/ML coursework, final-year projects, and faculty research.
Library & Knowledge Management
15
Library Migration — Luniva to KOHA
Full migration of the campus library from the legacy Luniva system to KOHA (open-source ILS), including OPAC customisation — branding, search, faceting — circulation rules, patron categories, item types, and MARC frameworks. Students and faculty get a modern, searchable, mobile-friendly catalogue with reservation and self-service. Maintained in-house with regular updates, database backups, and performance tuning.
16
Private DSpace Repository (E-Library)
A private DSpace digital repository for e-library resources, theses, project reports, faculty publications, and lecture material. Access is role-controlled — internal-only content stays within the campus network; open content is publicly accessible.
Administration & Store
08
Store Management System
Full lifecycle management of every store item — purchase order generation, goods-received entry, stock keeping, issue/store-out transactions, and disposal. Dashboard analytics cover consumption trends, low-stock alerts, department-wise utilisation, and vendor performance for the store keeper and administration.
